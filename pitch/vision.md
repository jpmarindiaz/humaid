# Vision

## Colombia is the launch site, not the destination

humaid is a **NASA Lifelines program project** with the **United Nations in Colombia** as a partner on data and AI governance and **indigenous water-rights organisations in Putumayo** at the grass-roots co-design level. That stack of relationships is unusual — and it tells you what we're building toward.

We are not building Colombia tech for Colombia. We are using Colombia as the **first deployment** of a humanitarian-response architecture meant to transfer to every flood-exposed region on Earth where the same five disconnects exist.

## Five-year arc

### Year 1 — Colombia La Mojana + Putumayo, knowledge-first

- Sentinel-1 SAR retrofit + first model fine-tune deployed.
- Community stations in 14 anchor municipalities (8 La Mojana + 6 Putumayo) — **co-designed with the indigenous water-rights organisations and NGO field partners already at the table**.
- Local app v1 in the hands of JAC presidents, *alcaldías*, ELC focal points, Cruz Roja seccionales.
- Q&A grows to ~1,500 pairs; full coverage of HRP/CPRP cluster sectors.
- First documented "alert-to-action" case study: a Cara de Gato breach where pre-synced households evacuated faster because of humaid.
- **UN Colombia** publishes its data and AI governance strategy with humaid as a worked-example reference case.

### Year 2 — Adjacent Colombia basins + first satellite payload

- Atrato (Chocó), Cauca low-basin, Magdalena-Bajo, Arauca-Casanare, Vichada-Orinoquía coverage.
- First **hosted-payload partnership** — humaid model on a shared satellite (CubeSat or commercial smallsat).
- Q&A expansion to wildfire (El Niño), drought, dengue/malaria outbreaks.
- Indigenous-language overlays: Embera-Chamí, Wayuunaiki, Inga, Kamëntsá co-developed with cabildos.

### Year 3 — Andean-region expansion

- Equator → Peru → Bolivia. Same flood mechanism (Andean-Amazon foothill flash floods + lowland riverine), same disconnects in the response chain.
- Co-localisation with Andean Community of Nations (CAN) civil-protection mechanism.
- Q&A pipeline becomes self-sustaining — local NGOs and civil-protection agencies contribute their own Q&A pairs through the same multi-agent template.

### Year 4 — Cross-hazard generalisation

- The architecture is **hazard-agnostic**. Same satellite payload handles wildfires (Liquid AI's wildfire-prevention cookbook is the proof), volcanic eruptions (the Mocoa risk diagnostic is partly volcanic), landslides, severe weather.
- Same local app surfaces hazard-specific Q&A bundles.
- Same community station hosts whatever knowledge base is relevant for *this* place.

### Year 5 — Global public-good infrastructure

- The full stack — model weights, Q&A schema, retrieval pipeline, community-station spec, local-app codebase — is **open-source under a permissive licence** with an institutional-licence overlay for sustained funding.
- "Build your own humaid for *your* river basin" becomes a documented, supported workflow.
- Partnerships with multilateral civil-protection mechanisms (UN OCHA, EU CPM, IADB).

## What humaid is *not* trying to be

- **Not a competitor to UNGRD or OCHA.** It is the **delivery rail** they have been missing.
- **Not a commercial flood-insurance underwriter.** That market is well-served by Cloud-to-Street, Floodbase et al. We complement; we don't compete.
- **Not a replacement for the response system.** It is a **glue layer** between the satellite, the institution, the responder, and the family.
- **Not a chatbot replacing experts.** Every answer cites a source PDF — humans remain accountable. We make the experts' knowledge *reachable*, not redundant.

## The Zenú parallel

The Zenú embraced the floods because they had **landscape-literacy delivered by living in the landscape**. Two thousand years of accumulated knowledge.

We won't get that back through brute force. But the loop we *can* close is the one between the modern equivalent of that knowledge — encoded in 17 PDFs, 471 Q&A pairs, satellite-resident inference, offline-first delivery — and the people who live with the water today.

If humaid does its job, the question "what do I do?" stops being a literacy gap and starts being **answerable in a sentence, in 10 seconds, on a phone with no signal**.

That is the win condition.
