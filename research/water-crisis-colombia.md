# Humanitarian aid in Colombia: water management, floods, and ENSO

Research note compiled from OCHA, UNGRD, IDEAM, ACAPS, CERF, FAO, UNICEF, IFRC, IOM, IISD, IASC, Copernicus / UNOSAT and academic sources. Companion files: [`links.md`](./links.md), [`download/`](./download) (PDFs), [`download-md/`](./download-md) (markdown conversions), [`humanitarian-aid-colombia/humanitarian-aid-context.md`](./humanitarian-aid-colombia/humanitarian-aid-context.md).

Scope: water-driven humanitarian crises in Colombia (floods, flash floods/avenidas torrenciales, ENSO-related drought/excess rainfall) with focus on **La Mojana** and **Putumayo**, plus the role of satellite imagery and Earth Observation (EO) for prevention and response.

---

## 1. Big picture — where water-driven crises hit Colombia

Colombia is exposed to two cyclical hydroclimatic drivers that dominate water-related humanitarian needs:

- **La Niña (cold ENSO phase)** — wetter than normal across the Andean, Caribbean and Pacific regions. Triggers riverine flooding, flash floods/avenidas torrenciales, landslides, and dam/levee failures. The 2010-2011 and 2021-2023 La Niña events are reference cases for nationwide flooding emergencies.
- **El Niño (warm ENSO phase)** — drier and warmer; drives forest fire risk, water shortages for human consumption and agriculture, and reduces hydropower generation.

The 2021-2023 La Niña was a tri-yearly "triple-dip" event. UNGRD's official damage assessment (`UNGRD-Evaluacion-La-Nina-2021-2023.pdf`) compiles the human and material impact across all departments. Colombia's `Plan Nacional de Gestión ante el Fenómeno El Niño` (`UNGRD-Plan-Nacional-El-Nino.pdf`) is the canonical national contingency plan for the warm phase.

Two regions concentrate recurrent water-driven humanitarian load:

| Region | Driver | Population dynamic |
|---|---|---|
| **La Mojana** (Sucre, Córdoba, Bolívar, Antioquia) | River confluence (Cauca + San Jorge + Magdalena), levee/dike failures (Cara de Gato, Los Arrastres) | 300+ communities, 166k+ people affected since Aug 2021 break |
| **Putumayo / Amazonia / Orinoquía** (Putumayo, Amazonas, Vichada, Caquetá, Arauca, Guaviare) | Andean-Amazon rainfall, river overflows (Putumayo, Caquetá, Mocoa, Mulato, Sangoyaco), avenidas torrenciales | Indigenous communities; 2025 rainy season alone: 16k in Putumayo, 10k in Amazonas, 11.5k in Vichada, 42.9k in Arauca |

## 2. La Mojana — a chronic, slow-onset flood crisis

### What it is

La Mojana is an alluvial floodplain in the Depresión Momposina where the Cauca, San Jorge and Magdalena rivers meet. Historically a wetland that absorbed seasonal flood pulses, it has been progressively degraded by deforestation, mining sediment, hydraulic infrastructure failures and inadequate territorial planning, so even modest rains now trigger inundation.

### Trigger event — 27 August 2021 onward

The breach of the **Cara de Gato dike** (San Jacinto del Cauca, Bolívar) in August 2021 turned La Mojana into an open emergency that has persisted for 4+ years. According to OCHA's June 2025 factsheet (`OCHA-La-Mojana-Factsheet-No1-19062025.pdf`):

- **300+ communities** affected since August 2021
- **166,000+ people** impacted across **11 municipalities**
- During 2021-2022 the Equipo Local de Coordinación (ELC) of Córdoba assisted 90,000+ people (food security/nutrition, health, WASH, protection, temporary shelter, education in emergencies, early recovery)
- In 2025 alone, ~30,000 people newly affected; the flooded footprint is now expanding into areas not historically affected
- 27 August 2025 saw a new breach in Cara de Gato that re-activated the emergency

### 2024 sub-cycle (April–June)

ACAPS's August 2024 briefing (`ACAPS-2024-La-Mojana-Flooding.pdf`) and OCHA's resilience report document a second crisis pulse in 2024:

- 21 April 2024 — heavy rains start; filtrations in the rebuilt Jarillón Cara de Gato (finished Feb 2024)
- 6 May 2024 — Jarillón Cara de Gato breaks again
- 8 May 2024 — Jarillón Los Arrastres breaks
- 27,039 people affected in San Jacinto del Cauca, Guaranda, Sucre, Majagual, San Benito Abad, San Marcos, Ayapel
- Calamidad Pública declared in Sucre dept and 7 municipalities (6-month validity)
- By 27 May 2024: ~850,000 ha flooded nationally; by 11 June 2024: 860,000 ha (vs 590,000 ha in the 2022 peak)

### Key humanitarian actors and processes

- **UNGRD** + Fondo Adaptación run the **Plan Mojana** — interinstitutional response with infrastructure works (e.g. ampliación del Canal de la Esperanza diverting ~60% of the Río Cauca flow) and humanitarian kit distribution.
- **ELC Córdoba** + **OCHA UMAIC** coordinate the local humanitarian architecture; ran the 2021-2022 response with 9,500 alimentary kits and CERF-funded interventions (`CERF-2022-La-Mojana-Rapid-Response.pdf` — Allocation 22-RR-COL-54690).
- **CERF** (UN's Central Emergency Response Fund) released rapid-response funds in 2022 specifically because of persistent La Niña + 3 dike ruptures.
- **UNICEF / FAO / OPS / World Vision / OCHA** ran an inter-agency mission in May 2024 documented in `Construyendo resiliencia en la Mojana` (community-prioritized recommendations, AAP/Flagship Initiative methodology).
- **Cruz Roja Colombiana / IFRC** activated DREF operations (e.g., MDRCO021).
- **Procuraduría General** ordered (Sept 2025) a special intervention demanding UNGRD adopt urgent humanitarian measures.

### Long-term resilience track

- **IISD** (`IISD-Restoring-Wetlands-La-Mojana.pdf`) — Sustainable Asset Valuation modeling concludes that wetland restoration outperforms grey infrastructure for flood mitigation in La Mojana.
- **UNDP-GCF Adaptation Fund / "Reducing Risk and Vulnerability to Climate Change in the Region of La Depresión Momposina"** — long-running adaptation project on climate-resilient water management.
- Community-led actor: **Alianza Común La Mojana** — 300+ JACs convened in Sincelejito (Majagual), now an interlocutor with UNGRD and Fondo Adaptación.

## 3. Putumayo — flash-flood-prone Andean–Amazon region

### Mocoa avalancha (31 March – 1 April 2017) — the reference catastrophe

Heavy convective rainfall over the headwaters of the Mocoa, Sangoyaco and Mulato rivers triggered an **avenida torrencial** that destroyed sectors of the urban area of Mocoa.

Documented outcome (per UNAL `UNAL-Catastrofe-Mocoa.pdf`, ACAPS 2017 briefing, OCHA SitReps and Mocoa's own 2023 risk diagnostic `Mocoa-DiagnosticoRiesgos-2023.pdf`):

- **335 fatalities**, **57 disappeared**, **400+ wounded**, **15,500+ damnificados** (Wikipedia/Cámara/UNAL convergent figures)
- 36 barrios + 13 veredas affected, 6 barrios totally destroyed
- Multi-actor response: UNGRD, FFAA, Bomberos, Defensa Civil, Cruz Roja, ELC Putumayo, OCHA, UMAIC, UNICEF, ACNUR, MSF
- Lessons (from research note already in repo): rapid assessment must be iterative; WASH and health are critical post-flood; albergue management requires integrated services; recovery must include risk reduction (cuencas, ordenamiento, EWS); donations need traceability

### 2025 rainy season — Putumayo + Amazonia + Orinoquía

OCHA SitRep No. 01 (29 July 2025, `OCHA-SitRep-Inundaciones-Amazonia-Orinoquia-2025.pdf`):

- **Putumayo**: 16,000+ affected; destruction of crops, bridges, aqueducts; high risk of further landslides and rises of Río Putumayo and Río Caquetá
- **Amazonas**: 10,000+ affected — Bora, Cocama, Huitoto, Inga, Miraña, Muinane, Ocaina, Ticuna, Yagua peoples; 2024 droughts had already weakened resilience
- **Vichada**: 11,500+ affected; Ríos Meta and Orinoco overflows; outbreaks of waterborne disease
- **Arauca**: 42,900+ affected; eight rivers in flood; campesino + indigenous + refugee/migrant population
- **Guaviare** + **Caquetá**: indigenous and rural communities; access difficulties slow response

### Concurrent armed-conflict layer

The OCHA Putumayo Departmental Briefing (`OCHA-Putumayo-Briefing-Departamental-2025.pdf`) is explicit that water emergencies compound on top of:

- GANE (Grupos Armados No Estatales) territorial control
- VBG/sexual violence risk (women, girls, women leaders)
- Risk of physical and cultural extermination of indigenous peoples (Bajo Putumayo, Leguízamo)
- Recruitment, use and utilization (RUU) of children/adolescents

This is the defining feature of Putumayo: every flood response must be designed for a context with simultaneous armed-conflict dynamics, restricted humanitarian access, and confinements.

## 4. ENSO — El Niño & La Niña processes and contingency plans

### National forecasting and warning chain

```
IDEAM (ENSO + hydrology forecasts)
   │
   ├── Comunicado ERFEN (monthly ENSO bulletin, joint with DIMAR/INVEMAR/SGC)
   ├── Boletín de Alertas Hidrológicas (BAH) — sub-zona hidrográfica × {alta, moderada, baja}
   └── Pronóstico de tiempo y clima
                 │
                 ▼
      UNGRD ── Circulares (e.g. Circular 028 de 2026 — alistamiento ante posible El Niño 2026)
        │     ── Plan Nacional de Gestión ante el Fenómeno El Niño
        │     ── Plan Mojana / Plan de contingencia La Niña
        │
        ▼
   CDGRD (depto) ── CMGRD (municipal) ── SAT locales
```

### Plans in place (PDFs in `download/`)

- `UNGRD-Plan-Nacional-El-Nino.pdf` — sectoral/territorial activation matrix for fires, water shortage, hydropower stress.
- `UNGRD-Evaluacion-La-Nina-2021-2023.pdf` — official damages and human impact assessment of the 2021-2023 triple-dip event.
- `IDEAM-Comunicado-ERFEN-Marzo.pdf`* — monthly ENSO advisories, with action recommendations including activating national/territorial contingency plans.

\* Direct PDF retrieval failed due to portal redirect; URL preserved in `links.md`.

### International anticipatory action linkages

- **IASC SOPs for Early Action to El Niño/La Niña** (`IASC-SOPs-Early-Action-El-Nino-La-Nina.pdf`) — global inter-agency procedure that maps probabilistic ENSO forecasts to early-action triggers.
- **FAO Anticipatory Action and Response Plan for La Niña — January 2025 update** (`FAO-La-Nina-Anticipatory-Action-Plan-Jan2025.pdf`) — global menu including agricultural advisories, early warning broadcasts, and pre-positioned inputs.
- **CERF Anticipatory Action window** — recurring funding channel for forecast-based early action.
- **OCHA Anticipatory Action priority** — global thematic window.

### What the EHP-Colombia uses

- `OCHA-HRP-Colombia-2024-2025-Summary.pdf` — Humanitarian Response Plan for Community Priorities (CPRP).
- `IOM-Colombia-Crisis-Response-Plan-2025.pdf` — IOM annual Crisis Response Plan.

## 5. Satellite imagery for prevention and response

This is the most actionable lever for technical work and the area where La Mojana has become a Latin-American pilot site.

### CopernicusLAC Panama Centre × UNGRD — operational EO services for Colombia

Per the CopernicusLAC blog (28 Apr 2026), three production services are integrated into UNGRD's GIS workflows under "Use Case 2 — Flood Frequency and Impact Mapping":

1. **Flood Extent Mapping** — automated Sentinel-1 SAR + Sentinel-2 ingestion → flood polygons within hours of image acquisition. Auto-publishes to authorised entity workspace on the CopernicusLAC platform.
2. **Flood Frequency Service** — pixel-level statistical record of how often each location has flooded over 2015-2025; distinguishes permanent vs temporary water bodies; informs spatial planning and land use.
3. **Flood Depth Service** — combines flood extent + DEM → estimated water depth → preliminary impact assessment cross-referenced with population, crops, livestock layers.

UNGRD reports three replicability factors:
- Open data + automated workflows + standard criteria → portable to Arauca, Orinoquía, etc.
- Consistent satellite coverage → cross-region comparability
- Native integration into existing institutional GIS → no new tooling required

Operational milestone: **Córdoba February 2026** flood event, where the services were used to support real-time emergency response (Copernicus EMS activation **EMSR865**).

### Copernicus EMS (European service)

- **EMSR865** — Severe flooding in Córdoba (Feb 2026); Rapid Mapping requested by Colombia. Charter triggered by UNOSAT.
- **EMSR286** — Ituango Dam risk of failure (May 2018) — Cauca river embankment dam, Antioquia.
- **EMSR847** — additional Colombia activation (referenced via UNOSAT product 4210).

Maps from these activations are publicly downloadable as georeferenced PDFs/TIFFs/JPEGs.

### UNOSAT (UN Satellite Centre)

- **UNOSAT product 4022** — November 2024 floods in Colombia, live web map.
- Triggered the International Disasters Charter in Nov 2024 for La Mojana / Caribbean coast floods.
- Generates damage proxy maps with InSAR for post-disaster building damage assessment.

### Sentinel-1 SAR for La Mojana (FAO DIEM + Sentinel Hub)

The FAO Data in Emergencies (DIEM) workflow used by the WFP/FAO storymap on the 2024 La Mojana floods illustrates a reproducible pipeline:

- Sentinel-1 SAR before/after pairs (25 Apr 2024 vs 31 May 2024) — radiometric terrain correction
- Cross-overlay with ESA WorldCover crop and grassland masks
- Per-municipality computation: 35% of San Benito Abad cropland flooded; 44% of Ayapel cropland flooded; 14% of Ayapel grassland; 32% of San Benito Abad grassland
- Time-lapse animations for May 2015–2024 to communicate trend

### Academic / methodological references

- *Detection and characterization of Colombian wetlands using ALOS PALSAR and MODIS imagery* — wetland baseline.
- *On Flood Detection Using Dual-Polarimetric SAR Observation* (MDPI Remote Sensing, 2025) — La Mojana confluence used as test dataset (San Jorge + Cauca + Magdalena wetland).
- *A methodology for multitemporal analysis of hydraulic dynamics* — Nechí, Colombia, SAR-based.
- *Multi-Temporal Sentinel-1 SAR and Sentinel-2 MSI Data for Flood Mapping* — combination yields reliable near-real-time flood mapping + damage assessment.

### Where satellite imagery has the most leverage

| Phase | Use case | Sensor / product |
|---|---|---|
| Preparedness | Flood frequency / hazard maps, exposure of crops & livelihoods, risk atlases | Sentinel-1 SAR archive, ESA WorldCover, Colombia Risk Atlas (UNGRD) |
| Anticipatory action | Forecast-based triggers cross-referenced with EO baselines | IDEAM hydrological forecasts + Sentinel-1 baseline |
| Emergency response (≤72h) | Rapid flood-extent maps, affected population estimates, infrastructure damage | Copernicus EMS Rapid Mapping, UNOSAT, CopernicusLAC Flood Extent Service |
| Monitoring (weeks-months) | Flood persistence and recession, secondary impacts (cropland, livestock, WASH infrastructure) | Sentinel-1 time series + DEM + Flood Depth Service |
| Recovery / DRR | Wetland degradation, deforestation, illegal mining, land-use change | Sentinel-2, Landsat, MODIS, ALOS PALSAR |

## 6. Humanitarian response process / workflow guidelines (consolidated)

The existing repo file `humanitarian-aid-colombia/humanitarian-aid-context.md` already lays out the Humanitarian Programme Cycle (HPC) phases applied in Colombia. Cross-references and additions:

- **Phase 0 (Preparedness)**
  - Plan Nacional de Gestión ante el Fenómeno El Niño (UNGRD)
  - Plan de contingencia La Niña / Plan Mojana
  - IASC SOPs for Early Action to El Niño/La Niña
  - FAO Anticipatory Action plans (commodity-specific)
  - IDEAM ERFEN bulletins → trigger to activate plans
  - SAT (Sistemas de Alerta Temprana) — IDEAM Boletín de Alertas Hidrológicas + local SATs (Barranquilla, Guajira, etc.)

- **Phase 1-3 (Rapid assessment → planning → mobilization)**
  - OCHA Reportes de Situación + Flash Updates (the Mocoa-2017 series is the textbook case)
  - UMAIC information products + ELC departamental briefings
  - Cluster activation (WASH, Health, Food Security & Nutrition, Protection, Shelter, EiE, Early Recovery, Coordination)
  - CERF Rapid Response and Anticipatory Action allocations
  - IFRC DREF operations
  - Unidad para las Víctimas — Atención y Ayuda Humanitaria Inmediata (AHI) for victims of armed conflict, applicable when floods compound on conflict (Putumayo, Catatumbo, Pacífico)

- **Phase 4 (Implementation)**
  - WASH (Cluster + UNICEF/PAHO + Acción contra el Hambre + Sahed)
  - Food security (FAO + WFP + Acción contra el Hambre, with cash/voucher modalities)
  - Health (PAHO/OPS + Cruz Roja + MSF in some contexts)
  - Shelter (NFI kits, alojamientos temporales, IFRC + UNHCR/ACNUR)
  - Protection (UNHCR, NRC, OPI, Defensoría)

- **Phase 5-6 (M&E + recovery)**
  - PDM (Post-Distribution Monitoring) per cluster
  - AAP / Flagship Initiative methodology — community priorities (Alianza Común La Mojana case)
  - Long-term: UNDP-GCF / Adaptation Fund projects, IISD wetland restoration recommendations, UNGRD infrastructure works

## 7. Open questions / gaps surfaced by the research

1. **No direct retrieval of IDEAM's monthly ERFEN PDFs** via curl — server returns redirects to a JS-rendered preview. A scrape via firecrawl works (URL preserved); a pipeline that polls the IDEAM site + parses ERFEN bulletins would enable automated trigger detection.
2. **Plan Mojana** (UNGRD's interinstitutional strategy) is referenced everywhere but does not have a single canonical PDF on the public portal (`portal.gestiondelriesgo.gov.co`) — content is spread across press releases. Worth a deeper dive if needed.
3. **CopernicusLAC Panama Centre Use Case 2 services** are restricted to "authorised entities" (UNGRD, IDEAM, FAN). Methodology is documented; access to the live workspace requires partnership.
4. **Forecast-based action triggers for Colombia** are not consolidated in a single document — they live across IASC SOPs, FAO AA plans, CERF AA allocations, and IDEAM forecasts. A consolidated Colombia-specific trigger matrix would be a useful product.
5. **Recovery vs reconstruction** in Mocoa (2017→ ongoing, see Cámara reference) and in La Mojana (Plan Mojana) are good comparative case studies for how DRR gets — or fails to get — embedded into reconstruction.
